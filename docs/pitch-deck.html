Problem

OTC settlement is still operationally primitive.

Counterparty risk

One side often moves first, creating delivery risk and expensive trust assumptions.

Chat-driven workflows

Telegram messages, screenshots, and manual instructions are standing in for actual settlement infrastructure.

No audit-grade receipt trail

Ops teams still reconstruct what happened after the fact instead of working from live state and linked transactions.

What desks need

  • bilateral escrow before release
  • clear state transitions
  • timeouts and dispute handling
  • verifiable receipts and tx history
Solution

A product, not just a contract.

Structured OTC deal creation

Party A defines exact assets, amounts, named counterparty, and timeout window in a clean front-end workflow.

Bilateral escrow funding

Both parties deposit before settlement, eliminating “who moves first?” risk.

Atomic settlement + receipt

Final settlement is executed on-chain and mapped back into a human-readable audit trail.

Workflow

Five-step bilateral settlement flow

1

Create

Party A creates a deal with exact trade terms and the named counterparty.

2

Accept

Party B reviews the deal and explicitly accepts before any funding begins.

3

Fund escrow

Each side independently funds its asset into the contract.

4

Confirm readiness

Both parties confirm they are ready to settle. No unilateral release.

5

Finalize

Settlement executes atomically and generates a receipt-grade activity record.

Product Experience

Two distinct roles, one coherent product journey

Party A experience

Create deal, define counterparty, set timeout, fund escrow, and monitor settlement state.

Party B experience

Accept deal, verify exact terms, fund escrow, confirm readiness, and finalize or dispute.

Operations experience

Use transaction history and receipt artifacts to verify what happened, when, and in which order.
